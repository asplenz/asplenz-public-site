## The real problem

One day, someone asks:

> "What exactly did the system know at the moment of that decision?"

### Regulatory audit
A regulator asks what happened 8 months ago. Your team spends weeks reconstructing a system state that no longer exists.

### Client dispute
A client contests an automated denial. No one can demonstrate the exact reasoning applied that day.

### Internal incident
The IT team must explain a decision made by a model that has since been updated three times.

**In these moments, after-the-fact explanation is no longer enough. You are asked for proof.**

## What fails today

### The truth disappears

* Systems change.
* Models evolve.
* Parameters are updated.
* Logs are fragmentary or derived.

### Reconstruction fails

* **Expensive**: teams mobilized for days or weeks.
* **Slow**: timelines incompatible with regulatory requirements.
* **Uncertain**: technical hypotheses impossible to verify.
* **Contestable**: conclusions that weaken your legal standing.

**You are forced to reconstruct the past instead of being able to prove it.**
